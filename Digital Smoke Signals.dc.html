// 12 · THE SECRET INGREDIENT

LoRa: small messages, very far

That quiet spot has a name: Long Range radio. A tiny bit of data — just text — sent for kilometres on almost no power.

2–10 km
from one device to the next
days
on a small battery — weeks with low-power mode or a little solar panel

only — and that's the point
No photos, no video, no endless scrolling. One look tells you what you need — then the phone goes back in the pocket. You save time, and you stay at the festival.
Bands and max power are set by regulation and differ by country — check your local rules.
// 13 · GOING DEEPER

It talks in chirps

LoRa = Chirp Spread Spectrum. Every bit is sent as a chirp — a tone sweeping across the band, exactly like a bird's call or a slide whistle. Our radios literally sing to each other.

Hears through noise
Heard even below the background hiss — that's the range.
Shrugs off chaos
Interference, reflections, a moving crowd — the sweep stays recognisable.
Nature's trick
Bats and radar use the same trick.

// 16 · CHOOSING YOUR CHIP

Buy the current generation

Cheap modules are everywhere. One rule: ESP32 + SX1262.

THE MICROCONTROLLER
MCU options
  • ESP32 — Heltec V3, T-Beam. The default.
  • nRF52 — RAK4631, T114. Weeks of battery.
  • Ready-made — Seeed T1000-E, Card Tracker. No fuss.
→ ESP32 — what most boards ship with.
THE RADIO
LoRa modules
  • SX1262 — current gen. Buy this.
  • SX1276/78 — older, still fine.
→ SX1262 — better range per milliwatt.

// 18 · THE SOFTWARE

Meet Meshtastic

Free, open-source software that turns cheap LoRa radios into your own private mesh. LoRa is the voice — Meshtastic is the language. And your phone? Just the keyboard.

  • No towers, no SIM card, no monthly plan
  • Connects to your phone over Bluetooth
  • Messages are encrypted — only your tribe can read them
  • Open source — it belongs to everyone

// 20 · WHY IT REALLY MATTERS

When they switch it off

A network with an owner has an off switch. Governments slow down or shut off the internet to silence people.

Iran, 2022 — the internet was shut down and throttled across the country during the protests, so people could not organise or be heard.

A mesh you own has no centre to take and no plug to pull. The network is the people.
